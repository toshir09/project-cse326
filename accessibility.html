Accessibility

Website design and structure

  1. Website Design and Structure: Ensure that the website design is clean and simple, with a clear navigation structure. Use heading tags (H1, H2, H3, etc.) appropriately to create a logical hierarchy of information.
Text and Font
  1. Use a readable font with sufficient contrast between text and background colors. Allow users to adjust the text size without loss of content or functionality.
Alternative text for imaages
  1. Provide alternative text (alt text) for images, charts, and graphs, allowing users with screen readers to understand the content.
Forms and Input Field
  1. Include clear and concise labels for form fields. Ensure that form fields are programmatically associated with their labels to assist screen reader users.
User Feedback and Testing
  1. Encourage users to provide feedback on the website's accessibility. Conduct regular accessibility testing with users who have disabilities to identify areas for improvement.
